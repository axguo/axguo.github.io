Hi, I'm Alicia!

I'm an HCI researcher, computational artist, and poet. Right now I am a first year master's student at MIT studying Computer Science interested in multi-modal generative models, tools for thought, and expanding human creativity and cognition through human-AI interaction.

Currently, I am working in MIT Media Lab's Fluid Interfaces group on exploring agency in working with generative models. This summer I was at ETH Zürich working with Dr. Christian Holz on emotion perception in VR. Previously, I was at CSAIL in Stefanie Mueller's lab working on an interface for creating non-digital animations with polarizers. Before that, I worked on detecting gender bias in language generation with the Data to AI Lab and exploring human ability to detect deepfakes with the Affective Computing group. My research interests include understanding language, computer-supported creative work, and social computing.

Some things that I care about are sustainability, language learning, and ethical and responsible design. Recently I've been exploring food poetry, building worlds with speculative fiction, and algorithmic art. If anything catches your eye, feel free to say hi! :)
